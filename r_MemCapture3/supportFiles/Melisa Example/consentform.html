Key information:

You are being asked to participate in a voluntary research study. The purpose of this study is to investigate attention, memory and decision making. Participating in this study may involve judging stimuli, or studying a series of stimuli and trying to remember them for a test. Your participation will last up to 50 minutes. There are no associated risks involved with participating in this study, other than those encountered in daily life. There are no direct benefits involved with participating in this study, other than the credit you will earn.

What you will do in this study:

You are being asked to be a participant in a research study about attention, memory, and decision making. In this study you will view a series of stimuli and may be asked to judge those stimuli or to remember them for a subsequent test. These stimuli may be objects, pictures, videos, words, or sentences, and you may be asked to make judgments about your ability to remember them. Prior to studying these stimuli, you may be given instructions specifying how the test will be carried out. You may be asked to interact with research assistants or other participants to work on some of the research tasks. You may also be asked to perform and make judgments about simple manual tasks such as moving a mouse to keep a cursor within a moving target on the computer screen or pressing buttons in a certain order and/or at a certain speed.

Voluntary withdrawal:

You must be 18 years or older to participate. Participation in this research is voluntary. You may quit the experiment at any point of the process without any penalty by simply closing the window.

Risks and benefits:

There are no anticipated risks, beyond those encountered in daily life, associated with participating in this study. By participating in this study, you will help to contribute to the advancement of cognitive psychological research. Results from these experiments may be presented at scientific meetings, and in published articles or books.

Compensation:

This experiment takes no longer than 50 minutes, and you will receive 1 credit for your participation. If you withdraw before completing the experiment, you may not receive credit. At the end of your participation you will receive an explanation of the study.

Research records:

In general, all collected data are kept in strict confidence and are only available to researchers immediately involved with this study. After the experiment is completed, we will analyze your results along with the results of many other participants, and not in terms of your individual performance. We will record your name only to make sure that you don't take part in the same experiment twice. Your de-identified information and data could be used for future research without additional informed consent.

Confidentiality of study-related information:

We will use all reasonable efforts to keep your personal information confidential, but we cannot guarantee absolute confidentiality. When this research is discussed or published, no one will know that you were in the study. But, when required by law or university policy, identifying information (including your signed consent form) may be seen or copied by: a) The Institutional Review Board that approves research studies; b) The Office for Protection of Research Subjects and other university departments that oversee human subjects research; or c) University and state auditors responsible for oversight of research.
